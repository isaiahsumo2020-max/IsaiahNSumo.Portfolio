My Journey

From Civil Compound to Valedictorian. A story of faith, resilience, and unbreakable determination.

I was born on July 5, 1999, at CB Dumba Hospital, Civil Compound Community, Gbarnga City, Bong County, Republic of Liberia. Born into the relationship of George Sumo and Hellena N. Sengbeh, both from the Kpelleh ethnic group. The "N" in my name stands for "Nyenian" – a Kpelleh name meaning "The World", given to me by my aunt, Annie T. Sumo.

2007

Childhood Struggles & Loss

Grew up in challenging circumstances after my parents separated. Survived serious illness during Liberia's post-civil crisis recovery. Raised by grandparents and aunts who depended on a sugarcane farm 1hr 10min walk away. My grandfather, Mr. Sumo B. Dolo, passed away June 8, 2008, before fulfilling his promise to enroll me in school.

2010

First Steps in Education

Started school at age 11 at Kingdom Garden (ABC) at Jorphenmue Public School. Survived another critical illness. Earned double promotions due to academic excellence. Completed elementary education through community support despite family tragedies including my grandmother's breast cancer diagnosis and aunt's sudden blindness.

2017

Life-Changing Move

At 18, moved to Montserrado County to live with uncle. Self-determination became my mantra: "I must stand firmly... God will fulfill His purpose for me." Overcame household challenges, engaged in small-scale trading (cheese, biscuits), attended Jawalah Community School and later Pipeline Junior & Senior High School.

2022

Self-Made Success

Self-funded education selling cold juice (20% commission), mosquito coils, mobile minutes, waist belts. Became DJ Bequizzy. Graduated as Valedictorian at Pipeline Junior & Senior High School. Awarded NOCAL National Scholarship for university.

2026

Building the Future

Currently pursuing BSc Information Technology (Networking & System Administration) at BlueCrest University Liberia. Training at Orange Digital Center in Web Development, IoT, TinyML. Building expertise in Cybersecurity and Graphic Design.

"My journey is a testament to resilience, faith, and determination. Despite early hardships, illness, and limited support, I relied on hard work, discipline, and self-reliance to pursue my education. I hope my story inspires others to never give up."
