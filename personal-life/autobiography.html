My Journey

A privacy-safe story of resilience, self-determination, and turning challenges into opportunity.

I was born in Liberia and raised in a close-knit community under challenging circumstances. My early life was shaped by family separation and economic hardship, which required me to grow resilient from a very young age. I was raised by my grandparents, aunts, and extended family members who played a critical role in my upbringing.

Growing up in a post-conflict environment, access to basic resources, including healthcare and education was limited. I experienced serious health challenges as a child, but through family support and faith, I recovered and continued forward. My grandparents sustained the household through small-scale farming, which became the foundation of our survival during difficult years.

Despite repeated setbacks and the loss of key family support figures, I remained committed to education. I began school later than many of my peers and advanced through elementary and secondary education through discipline, strong values, and the willingness to work for my own survival. Throughout my academic journey, I supported myself through small-scale trading and informal work while balancing school responsibilities.

Through perseverance, self-belief, and faith, I rose academically and graduated as Valedictorian, an achievement that earned me a national scholarship to pursue higher education.

Today, I am an Information Technology student specializing in Networking and System Administration, with interests in Cybersecurity, Web Development, and digital innovation. My journey reflects resilience, self-determination, and a commitment to building solutions that empower others.
